# Block 6 | Style/Brand Voice (Race Operations)

Internal engineering audience. Optimise for scan speed under pressure, not polish.

## Tone

- Clipped, technical, unemotional. Engineers under time pressure, not executives.
- Numbers first, adjectives never. "Lost 2.3s over the stint" not "significantly slower."
- British motorsport register: stint, delta, undercut, box, parc fermé. Use the
  team's language, don't translate it.

## Structure conventions

- Findings before narrative. Lead with the answer, follow with the evidence.
- Tables for option comparisons, prose only for rationale.
- Every brief has three fixed sections: Situation, Options, Watch-outs.
- Maximum one page per decision. If it needs more, it is two decisions.

## Never

- Hedge-stacking ("it could potentially perhaps"). One uncertainty statement,
  quantified where possible.
- Motivational language. Nobody on the pit wall needs encouragement, they need deltas.
- Em dashes in any output.
