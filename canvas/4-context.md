# Block 4 | Context

The balancing force. The real-world situation every AI output must respect.

## Industry situation

- Formula 1 operates under a cost cap. Headcount and spend are constrained, which makes
  productivity per person the primary lever. This is the strategic justification for
  the entire AI programme.
- The sporting calendar runs 24 race weekends per season across four continents.
  Decision cadence is brutal: strategy meetings happen Thursday to Sunday on track
  time zones, debriefs on Mondays, upgrades signed off Tuesdays.
- Regulations change annually. Institutional memory of why past decisions were made
  is a genuine competitive asset.

## Market conditions

- Sponsorship market is crowded; sponsors demand measurable ROI, not just logo space.
- Rival midfield teams (three direct competitors for P5 in the constructors')
  are investing in similar data capabilities. Parity is the baseline; the edge is
  in decision process quality.

## Current scenario

Mid-season. The team sits P6 in the constructors' championship, eight points behind
P5. A major title sponsor renewal decision is due within six weeks. An upgrade
package decision (allocate remaining cost-cap headroom to this season or next year's
car) is in live deliberation. Both decisions are being run through the decision
intelligence process with full ledger tracking.

## Operating constraints

- Trackside personnel limits mean remote factory support does the heavy analysis.
- Parc fermé rules freeze car changes at set times; some decisions have hard,
  non-negotiable deadlines.
- All AI use must respect FIA sporting and technical regulations and the cost cap's
  treatment of software and services.
